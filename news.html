Tackling hunger and food insecurity, one step at a time.

What Progress Has Been Made?

According to the Sustainable Development Goals Report, the number of people experiencing hunger and food insecurity has been increasing since 2015, worsened by the pandemic and climate change. In 2022, approximately 9.2% of the global population, or 735 million people, faced chronic hunger, which was 122 million more than in 2019. Additionally, about 2.4 billion people (29.6% of the world) were moderately or severely food insecure, indicating they lacked access to adequate food, which is 391 million more than in 2019.

Data

  • In 2022, an estimated 45 million children under the age of 5 suffered from wasting, 148 million had stunted growth, and 37 million were overweight.
  • Anemia in women aged 15 to 49 has remained concerning, staying around 30% since 2000.
  • Countries experiencing moderately to abnormally high food prices fell from 48.1% in 2020 to 21.5% in 2021.
  • Between 2015 and 2021, total aid to agriculture in developing countries increased by 14.6%, from $12.8 to $14.2 billion (in constant 2021 prices).
